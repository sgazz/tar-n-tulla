# Vision

## One-Sentence Pitch

Tar&Tulla is a mobile 2D physics platformer where two jumpers tied by an elastic rope climb upward through timing, tilt, and sling-like momentum.

## Core Fantasy

The player is not controlling one hero—they are managing a partnership in motion. Tar and Tulla move as a connected pair: one lands, the other swings; one pulls, the other follows. Success feels like learning a physical rhythm between two bodies and one rope.

## Target Platform

- **Primary:** iOS and Android (portrait orientation)
- **Input:** Touch, device tilt, and timing-based actions
- **Session length:** Short, repeatable runs (1–5 minutes)

## Intended Feeling

- **Physical** — Weight, stretch, and rebound should read clearly on screen.
- **Rhythmic** — Jump, land, pull, and recover in a repeating cadence the player can internalize.
- **Tense** — Small mistakes compound; the rope and gravity keep pressure on every decision.
- **Playful** — Bounces, near-misses, and sling recoveries should feel expressive, not punishing.

## What Makes the Game Different

Most vertical climbers reward a single avatar's reflexes. Tar&Tulla rewards coordination between two connected bodies. The elastic rope is not decoration—it is the main instrument. Momentum is shared, timing is doubled, and recovery often comes from sling physics rather than a simple second jump.

## Design Principles

1. **Physics first, feel second, content third.** If the rope and landing do not feel right, nothing else matters.
2. **Readable motion.** The player should always understand who is leading, who is trailing, and where the rope is under tension.
3. **Skill through rhythm, not memorization.** Levels should teach movement language, not scripted jump sequences.
4. **Fail fast, retry faster.** Death should be clear; restart should be immediate.
5. **Mobile-native input.** Tilt and touch must feel intentional, not bolted on.
6. **Restraint in scope.** One strong core loop beats many half-finished systems.
